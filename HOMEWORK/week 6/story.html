From HappyPlanetIndex.org I could see a list of countries, sorted by ranking of Happiness. There were several factors that contributed to the happiness index, but I thought it would be interesting to see how much of an impact a country's quality of life had on it. Therefore I compared the data from HappyPlanetIndex.org to data I found on Numbeo.

The world map shows a visualisation of the countries of which a happiness index score is known. They are color sorted, darker orange meaning the country has a high happiness index, while light orange shows that a country has a low happiness index. When hovering over a country, the precise index score is shown, while also its world ranking is shown.
The scatterplot shows how the happiness index is compared to the QoL score. When clicking on a country on the world map, only the dot of that country is shown on the scatterplot, so its respective values can easily be seen.

When looking at the comparison between the happiness index and the QoL score, you can see that countries with a high happiness index are leaning more torward the right side of the plot, meaning they have a high quality of life score. The are definitely outliers, such as Venezuela, which has a fairly high happiness index (ranked 29 in the world!), but a quality life score of 0. The political and economical crisis in Venezuela may be an explanation for this irregularity.
